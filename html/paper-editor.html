Molecular Graph Generation via
Transformer-based Architectures

Chen et al.
Abstract
We present a novel approach to molecular graph generation using transformer-based architectures. Our method encodes molecular structures as graph sequences and employs self-attention mechanisms to capture long-range dependencies between atoms and functional groups. The key innovation is the integration of graph positional encoding with multi-head self-attention. We evaluate on ZINC 250K and ChEMBL benchmarks.

1 Introduction

Molecular generation is a fundamental task in computational drug discovery. Traditional approaches rely on rule-based enumeration or variational autoencoders with string-based molecular representations [Gomez et al., 2018].

Recent advances in transformer architectures have opened new possibilities for sequence-based molecular generation. The self-attention mechanism is particularly well-suited for capturing the long-range dependencies inherent in molecular structures [Vaswani et al., 2017].

2 Method

2.1 Graph Sequence Encoding

We represent a molecular graph G = (V, E) as a sequence of atom tokens, where each atom v ∈ V is encoded with its atomic number, formal charge, and hybridization state.

2.2 Transformer Architecture

Our model consists of L = 6 transformer layers with h = 8 attention heads and hidden dimension d = 512. The attention mechanism computes:

Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QKT / √dk) V

3 Experiments

We evaluate on the ZINC 250K dataset.

· Validity: 99.2%
· Novelty: 95.8%
· Uniqueness: 99.7%

4 Conclusion

Transformer-based architectures offer a promising direction for molecular graph generation, achieving competitive results across standard benchmarks.
